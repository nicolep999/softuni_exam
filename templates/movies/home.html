Did You Know?

Movie Facts

Longest Movie Ever: The longest film ever made is "Modern Times Forever" (2011) at 240 hours (10 days)!

Silent Era: The first feature-length film was "The Story of the Kelly Gang" (1906) from Australia.

Behind the Scenes

Special Effects: The first CGI character in a movie was the "Genesis Effect" in Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan (1982).

Filming Secrets: Many famous movie scenes were filmed in just one take, including the iconic "Here's Johnny!" scene from The Shining.

Movie Statistics

Box Office: Avatar (2009) remains the highest-grossing film of all time with over $2.8 billion worldwide.

Production: The average Hollywood movie takes 2-3 years from concept to release, with filming typically lasting 3-6 months.
